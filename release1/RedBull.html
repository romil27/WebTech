About the Team Principal

Christian Horner started off as one of the youngest Team principals ever when he became the Team Principal of Red Bull Racing in 2005. Christian Horner has been the team principal of Red Bull ever since their inception, and he led the team to winning 4 consecutive Double championships. When he accompolished this, it was a record breaker, although this record has now been broken by Mercedes. From 2010-2013, Red Bull Dominated the series, wininng both the Constructors and Drivers Championships. Their star driver at the time was Sebastian Vettel, who later moved on to Ferrari. While they have faced tougher times in the Hybrid Era, due to a lack of a consistent power unit supplier, their partnership with Honda has proved to be useful, and has seen them rise to the top once again. In 2021, their driver Max Verstappen is currently fighting for the drivers' championships, and the RB16B, their current car, has proven to be superior than the Mercedes W12 in many ways.

Under his leadership, Red Bull Racing has:

Dominated for 4 seasons straight

Won 4 double world championships

Introduced a plethora of young drivers through their academy, many of whom are still racing in F1 today.

Managed to make a comeback in 2021, and are championship contenders this season.

Christian Horner has time and again proven to be a successful leader capable of putting the team at the top. While engine supplier issues have troubled them in the turbo hybrid era, they have found a permanent partner in Honda.
